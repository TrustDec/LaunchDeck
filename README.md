# LaunchDeck

LaunchDeck is a native macOS launcher built for people who miss the classic Launchpad.

It brings back the feeling of opening all your apps in one focused, fullscreen space: clean pages, large icons, folders, search, and fast app launching. The goal is simple: make browsing and opening apps on modern macOS feel smooth, visual, and enjoyable again.

## Why LaunchDeck

Apple moved away from the old Launchpad experience. If you preferred the original full-screen app grid, quick paging, and folder-based organization, LaunchDeck is designed to bring that workflow back.

Instead of another command launcher or menu bar utility, LaunchDeck is built around a visual, app-first experience:

- open a dedicated fullscreen launcher
- browse your apps across pages
- organize them into folders
- search when you need speed
- keep the experience focused on launching, not managing

## What It Feels Like

LaunchDeck is meant to feel closer to a system launcher than a regular app window.

- Fullscreen presentation that keeps attention on your apps
- Paged icon grid inspired by the classic macOS Launchpad
- Folder support for grouping related apps
- Lightweight search for jumping straight to an app
- Native macOS look and feel instead of a web-style dashboard

## Who It Is For

LaunchDeck is for Mac users who:

- liked the old Launchpad more than the new app browsing experience
- want a visual launcher instead of a keyboard-only workflow
- keep a large app collection and want cleaner organization
- want a launcher that feels at home on macOS

## Current Status

LaunchDeck is actively under development and already includes the core launcher experience: fullscreen browsing, paging, folders, search, and real app launching.

The project is focused on steadily improving fidelity, polish, animation, and interaction quality to get closer to the original Launchpad feeling while still fitting modern macOS.

## Vision

The long-term goal of LaunchDeck is not just to imitate an old feature, but to restore a style of interaction that many Mac users still prefer:

- visual
- immediate
- organized
- immersive

If you want Launchpad back, LaunchDeck is built for that.
